# TailorMyJob vs Jobscan: A 2026 Comparison

Two AI resume tools, two different bets. Jobscan is the established ATS scanner. TailorMyJob bundles resume tailoring with a Chrome extension and job-hunt automation at a fraction of the price.

## Why Compare Them

Both Jobscan and TailorMyJob promise the same outcome — get your resume past ATS filters and into a recruiter's queue. But they take very different approaches.

Jobscan's flagship product is a keyword match scanner that has been around since 2013 and is widely cited in career advice articles. TailorMyJob is a newer, AI-native platform that pairs resume analysis with a Chrome extension for autofill and a job-hunting module called Hunter.

If you only need a one-shot ATS score, Jobscan is a known quantity. If you want the full application workflow — score, rewrite, autofill, and track — TailorMyJob covers more of it for less money.

## Who Should Choose Jobscan

Pick Jobscan if:

1. You specifically want LinkedIn profile optimization
2. You trust the brand because you've seen it cited in major career publications
3. You only run an ATS scan a few times a year and the per-scan cost doesn't matter

Jobscan also has a longer track record of ATS-specific research and a larger published library of articles about applicant tracking systems.

## Who Should Choose TailorMyJob

## FAQ

### Is TailorMyJob really cheaper than Jobscan?

Yes. TailorMyJob Pro is $12/month and Premium is $25/month (which adds Hunter and the Chrome extension). Jobscan Premium is approximately $49.95/month for the monthly plan, dropping to roughly $16.66/month if you commit to an annual plan. For unlimited month-to-month use, TailorMyJob Pro is about 75% less. Pricing for both products may change — verify directly on each site before deciding.

### Does TailorMyJob actually score against an ATS like Jobscan does?

Yes. TailorMyJob runs your resume against the job description and returns a keyword match score, missing keywords, and concrete rewrite suggestions. The underlying model (AWS Bedrock with Amazon Nova / Claude) is different from Jobscan's proprietary scanner, but the output category is the same: a numeric match score plus a gap report.

### What does TailorMyJob offer that Jobscan doesn't?

Three things: a Chrome extension that helps autofill job applications and analyze JDs in-page, a job-hunting module (Hunter) that tracks applications and watchlists, and a multi-language UI (English, Traditional Chinese, Japanese, Spanish, Korean, French, German). Jobscan's strengths are its long track record and LinkedIn profile scoring, neither of which TailorMyJob currently competes with directly.

### Can I switch from Jobscan to TailorMyJob without losing my resume history?

Your resume itself is just a file — upload it to TailorMyJob and you're set up immediately. Jobscan's stored scan history doesn't transfer (no platform exposes that as portable data), but your master resume and cover letters are independent of either tool.

### Which one is better for non-English resumes?

TailorMyJob. The UI ships in seven languages and the analysis pipeline handles multilingual resumes. Jobscan's interface and primary keyword tooling is English-first.
