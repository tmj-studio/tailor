# How to Write an English Resume for Taiwan Job Seekers (2026 Guide)

If you are based in Taiwan and applying to multinational companies, overseas English-speaking roles, or US remote positions, your English resume is your single biggest leverage point. Most candidates from Taiwan submit a resume that is technically in English but reads like a translated Chinese CV — and that gap is exactly why qualified people get filtered out before a recruiter ever opens the file.

This guide is the condensed version of how to fix that.

## Why English Resumes Matter Specifically in Taiwan

There are three career paths that converge on the same requirement:

1. **MNCs in Taiwan** — Google Taipei, Microsoft, AWS, Salesforce, Stripe, and most foreign banks all run their entire pipeline (Workday or Greenhouse) in English, even when the role is based in Taipei.
2. **Overseas English-speaking roles** — Singapore, Hong Kong, Tokyo (foreign branches), Sydney, and London are the most common destinations for Taiwanese tech and PM talent.
3. **US remote** — GitLab, Automattic, Stripe Remote, Shopify, and Zapier compete for global talent. APAC time-zone coverage is an advantage, but you are competing with candidates worldwide.

Your English resume is the entry ticket for all three.

## Five Differences Between Chinese and English Resumes

This is where most Taiwan candidates get blindsided.

**1. Length.** US resumes are strictly **one page** for anyone with under 10 years of experience. Two pages only for senior directors and above. Chinese resumes regularly run three to four pages — that habit will sink you in the US market.

**2. Quantification.** English resumes require numbers in nearly every bullet. "Managed important clients with strong results" is filler. "Owned 25+ enterprise accounts generating $3.2M ARR at 94% renewal" is a resume bullet.

**3. Tone.** Drop the subject ("I"). Lead every bullet with a strong past-tense verb: Led, Built, Shipped, Scaled, Reduced, Drove, Architected, Owned. Avoid "Responsible for" — it is the single most overused phrase from non-native writers.

**4. Format.** No photo. No two-column layouts. No skill bars or icons. Use a single-column ATS-friendly template with standard fonts. See our [resume format guide](/blog/resume-format-guide) for the full layout rules.

**5. Outcome over duty.** Chinese resumes list duties; English resumes list outcomes. Every bullet should answer: what did you do, how did you do it, and what was the measurable result.

## How ATS Works at MNCs Hiring in Taiwan

Workday (used by Microsoft, Salesforce, Adobe), Greenhouse (Stripe, Airbnb, HashiCorp), and Lever (Netflix, Lyft) all do the same three things: parse your file into structured fields, match keywords against the job description, and rank candidates before a recruiter sees the list.

Two practical implications:

- If your formatting breaks the parser (two columns, text boxes, icons representing skills), the recruiter sees a corrupted version of your resume — not the polished PDF you uploaded.
- Keyword match is determined by the words you use, not the words you mean. If the JD says "project management" and you wrote "led cross-functional initiatives," the system does not connect them.

## The Five Mistakes I See Most From Taiwan Candidates

1. **Direct translation from Chinese.** "Responsible for", "Successfully completed", and "Helped with" are dead giveaways of a translated resume. Replace with strong verbs and concrete numbers.
2. **Adding a photo.** US recruiters frequently delete resumes with photos to avoid discrimination liability. Skip the photo unless you are applying in Germany, France, or Switzerland.
3. **Wrong file format.** Stick to PDF or DOCX. Never submit a Pages file, an image, or a ZIP.
4. **Personal info overload.** Drop height, weight, marital status, ID number, and military service. Keep name, email, phone with country code, LinkedIn, city, and (for engineers) GitHub or portfolio.
5. **GPA and student-era content for experienced candidates.** If you have three or more years of work experience, remove GPA, clubs, and student awards.

## Quantifying Achievements: Four Patterns That Always Work

- **Percentages**: "Increased conversion rate by 34% through A/B testing"
- **Dollar amounts**: "Generated $1.8M in new ARR" or "Saved $420K in annual costs"
- **People or scale**: "Led a team of 8 engineers across 3 time zones"
- **Time**: "Reduced deployment time from 4 hours to 12 minutes"

If a resume bullet has zero numbers, rewrite it.

## A Unique Angle: The Taiwan-Specific Trust Gap

One thing US recruiters quietly factor in: title inflation outside their familiar markets. A "Senior Manager" in some Asian markets maps to "Manager" or even "Senior Specialist" in US convention. You can defuse this by adding scope context to your title line — for example, "Senior Manager, Marketing (managed team of 6, $2.5M budget)". This single line short-circuits the recruiter's mental adjustment and gets you scored at the level you actually performed at.

## A Second Angle: LinkedIn-Resume Parity

Foreign recruiters cross-check LinkedIn against your resume within 30 seconds. Inconsistent titles, dates, or company names trigger an instant credibility hit. Every time you update your resume, mirror the change on LinkedIn. This is non-negotiable for MNC and remote applications.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Should I include a photo on my English resume?

No. US recruiters routinely delete resumes with photos to avoid discrimination liability. The only exceptions are traditional industries in Germany, France, and Switzerland. Even MNC recruiters based in Taiwan generally prefer no photo on English resumes.

### How long should an English resume be?

One page for anyone with fewer than 10 years of experience. Two pages only if you are a senior director or above, or applying with a UK-style CV. Going over one page as a mid-level candidate is one of the fastest ways to get filtered.

### Can I apply to MNCs without prior MNC experience?

Yes, and many MNC hires in Taiwan come from local companies. The key is reframing your local experience using English resume conventions: strong action verbs, quantified outcomes, and titles mapped to MNC equivalents. Pair this with a polished LinkedIn and English interview prep.

### Do ATS systems understand Chinese resumes?

Workday, Greenhouse, and Lever have limited Chinese support — they can parse the file but keyword matching is unreliable. If the JD is in English, always submit an English resume, even for MNC roles based in Taiwan, since internal review tools and global hiring committees operate in English.

### Is a cover letter required for MNC applications?

Most postings do not require one, but a short, sharp cover letter measurably improves response rates. Use a three-paragraph structure: why this company, two or three quantified achievements, and a clear call to action. Keep the total under 250 words.

### Should I use PDF or DOCX?

PDF is the safe default — formatting will not shift between systems. DOCX can parse more reliably on older ATS platforms. Prepare both and submit whichever the application portal requests. Never submit images, Pages files, or ZIP archives.
